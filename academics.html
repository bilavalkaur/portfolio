Projects

Buggy Design

Successfully designed and implemented an innovative buggy project that showcased both autonomy and obstacle detection capabilities. The buggy navigated a black line track independently, utilizing ultrasonic sensors to identify and halt at obstacles along its path. Leveraging IR sensors, we fine-tuned the buggy's ability to maneuver the track seamlessly. In our final evaluation, the buggy completed two laps and autonomously parked itself at the designated stop, adeptly responding to any encountered obstacles. This was achieved using an Arduino UNO board using Arduino IDE for the programming. This course also required circuit building on PCB board using Eagle software.
I achieved an overall A- grade in the course, reflecting not only technical proficiency in the project but also a commitment to excellence and problem-solving.

Mangonel Design

Engaged in a multidisciplinary project that demanded a fusion of mechanical and electronics expertise. Leveraging skills in Arduino (IDE) and fundamentals of C programming, we embarked on a mission to measure the trajectory range of a mangonel and assess the angular velocity of its throwing arm.
For the conclusive assessment, we were tasked with precision throws at specified distances of 25m, 20m, and 15m using the throwing arm. This hands-on evaluation not only tested our project's functionality but also showcased its real-world applicability.

Truss Design

We built a truss bridge using wood and wires whose efficiency was calculated considering factors like cost, material used etc.

Wastewater Treatment Plant Design

We designed a wastewater treatment plant for the metallurgy industry, calculating the conent of COD, BOD and other metallic pollutants in the industrial wastewater.
We calculated the size and capacity of the machinery required for it's treatment after carrying out research on the specific wastewater and assuming certain missing data through hit and trial.

Internships/Volunteering

Balmer Lawrie & Co. Ltd., Chennai

I delved into the intricacies of oil fatliquor, a crucial element in enhancing leather quality. I gained insights into the detrimental effects of natural animal fat on leather's lifespan and quality, and discovered the pivotal role of fatliquor as a substitute for this traditional component while imparting softness to the leather.
During this exploration, I specifically focused on Balmol SFL (Synthetic Fat Liquor), an anionic fatliquor crafted from paraffin wax and special esters. Balmol stands out as a light brown to reddish liquid, demonstrating exceptional compatibility with like-charged fatliquors and retanning agents. Its versatility extends to seamless integration with non-ionic products. Noteworthy features include a stable translucent emulsion and a pH range of 6.5-7.5 for its 10% emulsion.
